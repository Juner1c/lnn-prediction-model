# Frontend Design

Approach this as the design lead at a small studio known for giving every client a visual identity that could not be mistaken for anyone else's. Make deliberate, opinionated choices about palette, typography, and layout that are specific to this brief.

## Ground it in the subject

Pin down what the product or subject is before designing: name one concrete subject, its audience, and the page's single job. The subject's own world, its materials, instruments, artifacts, and vernacular, is where distinctive choices come from.

## Design Principles

1. **The Hero is a Thesis**: Open with the most characteristic element in the subject's world (e.g. live telemetry map, dynamic Heat Index gauges, thermal risk gradient status).
2. **Typography Carries Personality**: Pair display and body faces deliberately (e.g., Outfit / Space Grotesk / Inter), with clear type scaling and intentional weight contrasts.
3. **Structure is Information**: Use structural devices (dividers, telemetry badges, spatial distance cards) that encode true information about station clusters and thermal risks.
4. **Leverage Motion Deliberately**: Smooth transitions, live gauge updates, glow animations on high heat index alerts, micro-interactions on station selection.
5. **Rich Aesthetic & Palette**: Dark meteorology theme (`#0B0F19` deep atmospheric navy, `#00F2FE` telemetry cyan, `#FF4E50` extreme heat alert coral, `#1B2438` glassmorphism card surfaces).
6. **No Placeholders**: Production-grade HTML, CSS, JavaScript dashboard with Chart.js, Leaflet interactive map, and real microservice API integration.
